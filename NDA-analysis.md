## Our NDA Posture

Turtle Advisors (operating as Invest Movement Pte. Ltd.) is almost always the **Receiving Party** evaluating a potential investment or acquisition. Our template is deliberately lean — 7 clauses, plain language, SIAC arbitration, 2-year term, Jerry signs as Investment Director. Designed to get signed fast without negotiation friction.

When counterparties supply their own paper, we review against our template and precedents (Fey Day, Multron, EngageRocket, FoodX, Stamford, UnUsUaL) and push back where needed.

## Standard Rebuttal Checklist

When reviewing a counterparty NDA, check for:

### Always Push Back (Critical)

1. **Standard exclusions from Confidential Information** — must include at minimum: (a) public domain, (b) prior knowledge, (c) independent development, (d) third-party rightful receipt, (e) legal compulsion with prompt notice and minimum necessary disclosure. If missing, non-negotiable.

2. **Backup retention carve-out on return/destruction** — right to retain copies required by law, regulation, or standard backup/compliance procedures, subject to ongoing confidentiality. Present in our template and all signed NDAs.

3. **Legal compulsion / regulatory disclosure carve-out** — if not already captured in the exclusions, must have a standalone clause permitting disclosure under law/court order with prompt notice and minimum disclosure. Essential for MAS and other regulatory obligations.

### Push Back Where Appropriate (Moderate)

4. **SIAC arbitration over Singapore courts** — our template uses SIAC (single arbitrator, English, Singapore seat). Especially important when counterparty is a foreign entity (e.g., Indonesian company in Project Gear) because arbitral awards are enforceable under the New York Convention. For Singapore-to-Singapore NDAs, courts are lower risk but still flag for consistency.

5. **Non-compete / unfair competition language** — reject any clause that extends beyond confidentiality into competitive restrictions. Our mandate requires freedom to evaluate and invest in businesses across sectors. The standard use restriction (Purpose only) is sufficient.

6. **Early termination mechanism for 2-year NDAs** — per Jerry's guidance: for NDAs with a 2-year term, include a termination on **5 business days' written notice**, consistent with Stamford precedent. NDAs with 1-year terms (Multron, UnUsUaL) don't need this because the exposure is naturally short.

7. **Written demand only for return/destruction** — reject oral demand triggers. No paper trail = disputes about whether demand was made.

8. **Internal work product carve-out** — reject requirements to destroy Turtle's own IC memos, analyses, and notes. Conflicts with internal compliance and record-keeping obligations.

### Flag but Likely Accept (Low)

9. **Reverse engineering prohibition** — standard in tech/manufacturing NDAs. Accept unless it could interfere with technical due diligence, in which case request a carve-out for authorised advisors.

10. **Injunctive relief** — one-directional (Discloser only) is standard and acceptable for a one-way NDA.

11. **Data protection / PDPA compliance** — good clause if present. Consider adopting in our template.

## Key Lessons from Jerry's Feedback (Project Gear)

### 1. Exclusions Must Be Appended, Not Replace the Definition

> "On the Information definition at Cl. 1.2, looks like the original definition got replaced by the carve-outs rather than the carve-outs being added after it. As it stands there's no positive definition of what Information actually is — just a list of what it isn't. We need both."

**Rule:** When adding exclusions to a counterparty's Confidential Information definition, always **retain the original positive definition** and append the exclusions after it. Never replace one with the other. The NDA needs both: what information IS covered, and what it ISN'T.

### 2. Early Termination Tracks with Term Length

> "Looking at our precedents, for 2-year NDAs (FoodX, Stamford, our own template) we tend to have an early termination mechanism. The ones without it (Multron, UnUsUaL) were both 1-year terms where the exposure is naturally short."

**Rule:**
- **2-year NDAs** → include early termination on 5 business days' written notice (Stamford precedent)
- **1-year NDAs** → early termination is optional; short exposure makes it less critical

### 3. Always Fill In Party Details as Tracked Changes

> "The Receiving Company blanks are still empty. Would be good to fill in those as tracked changes (entity name, UEN, address, signatory) — I usually do that so that the seller/company gets an understanding of who we are."

**Rule:** When marking up a counterparty NDA, always fill in Turtle Advisors' details in the Receiving Party blanks using tracked changes:
- **Entity:** TURTLE ADVISORS PTE. LTD.
- **UEN:** 202510946M
- **Address:** 137 Telok Ayer Street, #08-01, Singapore 068602
- **Signatory:** Jerry Tan, Investment Director

This gives the counterparty immediate visibility on who they're dealing with.

### 4. Enforceability Argument for SIAC

When proposing SIAC arbitration over courts, frame it as a **benefit to both parties**, not just a Turtle preference:
- Arbitral awards under SIAC are directly enforceable in the counterparty's home jurisdiction under the New York Convention
- Singapore court judgments may require a fresh proceeding for enforcement in foreign jurisdictions
- Arbitration is private and confidential — protects deal information

### 5. Process: Internal Review Before External Send

> "Going forward, do share with me first before sending NDA markups out. These are usually our first substantive touchpoint with a counterparty so I want to make sure we're aligned before anything goes across."

**Rule:** All NDA markups must go to Jerry for review **before** being sent to the counterparty. NDAs are the first substantive touchpoint and set the tone for the relationship.
