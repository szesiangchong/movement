## Our NDA Posture

Turtle Advisors (operating as Invest Movement Pte. Ltd.) is almost always the **Receiving Party** evaluating a potential investment or acquisition. Our template is deliberately lean — 7 clauses, plain language, SIAC arbitration, 2-year term, Jerry signs as Investment Director. Designed to get signed fast without negotiation friction.

When counterparties supply their own paper, we review against our template and precedents (Fey Day, Multron, EngageRocket, FoodX, Stamford, UnUsUaL) and push back where needed.

## Standard Rebuttal Checklist

When reviewing a counterparty NDA, check for:

### Always Push Back (Critical)

1. **Standard exclusions from Confidential Information** — must include at minimum: (a) public domain, (b) prior knowledge, (c) independent development, (d) third-party rightful receipt, (e) legal compulsion with prompt notice and minimum necessary disclosure. If missing, non-negotiable.

2. **Backup retention carve-out on return/destruction** — right to retain copies required by law, regulation, or standard backup/compliance procedures, subject to ongoing confidentiality. Present in our template and all signed NDAs.

3. **Legal compulsion / regulatory disclosure carve-out** — if not already captured in the exclusions, must have a standalone clause permitting disclosure under law/court order with prompt notice and minimum disclosure. Essential for MAS and other regulatory obligations.

### Push Back Where Appropriate (Moderate)

4. **SIAC arbitration over Singapore courts** — our template uses SIAC (single arbitrator, English, Singapore seat). Especially important when counterparty is a foreign entity (e.g., Indonesian company in Project Gear) because arbitral awards are enforceable under the New York Convention. For Singapore-to-Singapore NDAs, courts are lower risk but still flag for consistency. **When the counterparty insists on their home jurisdiction's governing law** (e.g., NSW Australia in AVS), propose SIAC arbitration under their governing law as a compromise — SIAC awards are enforceable in most jurisdictions under the New York Convention, so this gives both parties a neutral forum without forcing a change in substantive law.

5. **Non-compete / unfair competition language** — reject any clause that extends beyond confidentiality into competitive restrictions. Our mandate requires freedom to evaluate and invest in businesses across sectors. The standard use restriction (Purpose only) is sufficient.

6. **Early termination mechanism for 2-year NDAs** — per Jerry's guidance: for NDAs with a 2-year term, include a termination on **5 business days' written notice**, consistent with Stamford precedent. NDAs with 1-year terms (Multron, UnUsUaL) don't need this because the exposure is naturally short.

7. **Written demand only for return/destruction** — reject oral demand triggers. No paper trail = disputes about whether demand was made.

8. **Internal work product carve-out** — where the NDA already has a backup retention carve-out, a separate internal work product carve-out is likely overkill. Only push for a standalone work product carve-out if there is NO backup retention carve-out at all AND the return/destruction clause explicitly requires destroying internally-generated analyses and IC memos (e.g., Project Gear).

### Housekeeping (Always Do)

9. **Fill in Turtle Advisors party details as tracked changes** — entity name, UEN, address, signatory (Jerry Tan, Investment Director). Applies to preamble, notice section, and signature block. Don't leave blanks.

10. **Update stale effective dates** — counterparty NDAs may have been circulating for months or years (e.g., Project Aqua had "[xx] May 2023" — marketed since 2023). Always tracked-change the effective date to the actual signing date.

11. **Check the use restriction actually permits deal evaluation** — especially in mutual NDAs. The use clause must reference a defined Purpose (e.g., evaluating a potential investment/transaction). Reject open-ended authorisation mechanisms that require separate post-signing written consent (e.g., AVS clause 3.4).

### Flag but Likely Accept (Low)

12. **Reverse engineering prohibition** — standard in tech/manufacturing NDAs. Accept unless it could interfere with technical due diligence, in which case request a carve-out for authorised advisors.

13. **Injunctive relief** — one-directional (Discloser only) is standard and acceptable for a one-way NDA.

14. **Data protection / PDPA compliance** — good clause if present. Consider adopting in our template.

## Key Lessons from Jerry's Feedback (Project Gear)

### 1. Exclusions Must Be Appended, Not Replace the Definition

> "On the Information definition at Cl. 1.2, looks like the original definition got replaced by the carve-outs rather than the carve-outs being added after it. As it stands there's no positive definition of what Information actually is — just a list of what it isn't. We need both."

**Rule:** When adding exclusions to a counterparty's Confidential Information definition, always **retain the original positive definition** and append the exclusions after it. Never replace one with the other. The NDA needs both: what information IS covered, and what it ISN'T.

### 2. Early Termination Tracks with Term Length

> "Looking at our precedents, for 2-year NDAs (FoodX, Stamford, our own template) we tend to have an early termination mechanism. The ones without it (Multron, UnUsUaL) were both 1-year terms where the exposure is naturally short."

**Rule:**
- **2-year NDAs** → include early termination on 5 business days' written notice (Stamford precedent)
- **1-year NDAs** → early termination is optional; short exposure makes it less critical

### 3. Always Fill In Party Details as Tracked Changes

> "The Receiving Company blanks are still empty. Would be good to fill in those as tracked changes (entity name, UEN, address, signatory) — I usually do that so that the seller/company gets an understanding of who we are."

**Rule:** When marking up a counterparty NDA, always fill in Turtle Advisors' details in the Receiving Party blanks using tracked changes:
- **Entity:** TURTLE ADVISORS PTE. LTD.
- **UEN:** 202510946M
- **Address:** 137 Telok Ayer Street, #08-01, Singapore 068602
- **Signatory:** Jerry Tan, Investment Director

This gives the counterparty immediate visibility on who they're dealing with.

### 4. Enforceability Argument for SIAC

When proposing SIAC arbitration over courts, frame it as a **benefit to both parties**, not just a Turtle preference:
- Arbitral awards under SIAC are directly enforceable in the counterparty's home jurisdiction under the New York Convention
- Singapore court judgments may require a fresh proceeding for enforcement in foreign jurisdictions
- Arbitration is private and confidential — protects deal information

### 5. Process: Internal Review Before External Send

> "Going forward, do share with me first before sending NDA markups out. These are usually our first substantive touchpoint with a counterparty so I want to make sure we're aligned before anything goes across."

**Rule:** All NDA markups must go to Jerry for review **before** being sent to the counterparty. NDAs are the first substantive touchpoint and set the tone for the relationship.

## Additional Lessons (AVS / Project Aqua)

### 6. Backup Retention Covers Internal Work Product

> Seth's call on AVS: "Remove the internal work product carve-out since it seems like overkill for the same thing."

**Rule:** Where a backup retention carve-out is being added, a separate internal work product carve-out is redundant. Only push for both when the NDA has zero retention carve-outs AND explicitly requires destruction of internally-generated materials (e.g., Project Gear clause 2.3).

### 7. SIAC Under Foreign Governing Law as Compromise

> Seth's call on AVS: "Let's propose SIAC arbitration under NSW law as a compromise, given that SIAC awards are enforceable in Australia under the New York Convention."

**Rule:** When the counterparty insists on their home jurisdiction's governing law, don't fight the substantive law — fight the forum. Propose SIAC arbitration under their governing law. This gives Turtle a neutral, confidential forum with internationally enforceable awards, while respecting the counterparty's preference on substantive law. Sequence: (1) Singapore law + SIAC (ideal); (2) their law + SIAC (compromise); (3) their law + their courts (last resort).

### 8. Watch for Unworkable Use Restrictions in Mutual NDAs

The AVS NDA (clause 3.4) restricted use to purposes "the Disclosing Party may after the date of this Agreement expressly authorise in writing" — meaning zero permitted use without a separate post-signing authorisation. Always check that the use restriction references a defined Purpose (e.g., evaluating a potential investment/transaction), not a blank authorisation mechanism.

### 9. Execution Mechanics for Foreign Counterparties

The AVS NDA uses s.127 Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) execution, which requires two directors or director + secretary. Turtle's standard single-signatory approach (Jerry alone) may not satisfy this. Flag execution format early when dealing with Australian counterparties.

## Key Lessons from Jerry's Feedback (Project Aqua)

### 10. Always Update Stale Effective Dates

> Jerry on Aqua: "Wow they have been marketing since 2023"

**Rule:** When a counterparty NDA has a placeholder or stale effective date (e.g., `[xx] May 2023` in a deal being negotiated in 2026), always update it to the current date as a tracked change. A stale date suggests the NDA template has been recycled from an earlier process — flag this to Jerry as it may indicate the deal has been shopped around.

### 11. Tracked-Change Every Substantive Edit — Don't Just Comment

> Jerry on Aqua clause 2.3: "Was there a deletion of 'oral'? May want to mark it up as a change, as I don't see it"

**Rule:** Every substantive change must appear as a **tracked change** (strikethrough/insertion) in the document, not just as a comment. Comments explain the reasoning; tracked changes show the counterparty exactly what's being proposed. If a comment says "we request X be removed" but the text isn't actually struck through, the counterparty may miss it. Always pair the comment with the corresponding tracked edit.

### 12. Don't Quote-Wrap Inserted Contract Language

> Jerry on Aqua clause 7: "I'd remove the "" in your Clause 7 markups"

**Rule:** When inserting new contract language as a tracked change, do NOT wrap it in quotation marks ("" or ""). The inserted text is meant to become part of the agreement — quotation marks make it look like a suggestion being discussed rather than proposed operative language. The tracked-change formatting (underline/colour) already signals that it's a proposed addition.

### 13. Operator NDAs Must Be Mutual

> Jerry on Maxwell: "I wouldn't have cleared the doc for sharing with him as there's a few gaps — I'd wanted our one-pager to be converted to a mutual NDA, but the current draft is still structurally one-way."

**Rule:** When Turtle/Movement is sharing information **with** an operator (not receiving from a target), the template must be converted to a **mutual NDA** before sending. The standard template only protects Turtle's information as Discloser — but in operator relationships (co-investment, deal sharing), both parties disclose. "Disclosing Party" and "Receiving Party" must become role-based labels applying to whichever side is sharing, with symmetric obligations. Use EngageRocket or FoodX as mutual NDA precedents. Also verify the Purpose clause reflects the actual relationship (co-investment/deal sharing, not investment evaluation).

## Process Checklist (Pre-Send)

Before sending any NDA markup to a counterparty, verify:

1. ☐ All comments have corresponding tracked changes (not just comment-only suggestions)
2. ☐ Party details filled in as tracked changes (entity, UEN, address, signatory)
3. ☐ Effective date is current (not a stale placeholder from a prior process)
4. ☐ Inserted contract language has no quotation marks wrapping it
5. ☐ Exclusions are appended to the definition, not replacing it
6. ☐ Early termination clause included if 2-year term (5 BD written notice)
7. ☐ Jerry has reviewed before sending to counterparty
